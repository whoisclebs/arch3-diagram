# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers pledge to make participation in
the arch3-diagram community a harassment-free experience for everyone,
regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex
characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience,
education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race,
religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- showing empathy and kindness toward other people
- being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- taking responsibility, apologizing, and learning from mistakes
- prioritizing the health of the community over individual ego

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- sexualized language or imagery and unwanted sexual attention or advances
- trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- public or private harassment
- publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- attempting to bypass moderation or safety mechanisms
- any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional open source environment

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of
acceptable behavior and will take fair, proportionate corrective action in
response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or
harmful.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject
comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are
not aligned with this Code of Conduct.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces and whenever an
individual is officially representing the project in public spaces.

## Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be
reported through the project repository:

- https://github.com/whoisclebs/arch3-diagram/issues
- https://github.com/whoisclebs/arch3-diagram/security/policy

If the report involves a security-sensitive matter, follow the process in
`SECURITY.md` instead of opening a public issue.

All reports will be reviewed promptly and handled with as much privacy as
possible.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders may use the following consequences:

1. **Correction**: private feedback and clarification
2. **Warning**: explicit warning and boundaries for future participation
3. **Temporary ban**: time-boxed removal from community spaces
4. **Permanent ban**: removal from project participation for repeated or severe
   violations
